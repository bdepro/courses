Consulting Team LabsData Intensive 35%
Wednesday lab sessions are where the Sustainable Alamance project gets built. Working in teams, you take on a real-world problem for an actual client—defining the issue, gathering and cleaning data, analyzing options, and developing recommendations. You receive structured feedback at key milestones throughout the semester, not just at the end. The final presentation goes to a live client audience in Week 12.

Phase 1 Milestone — Core Numbers Update
Recalculate recidivism savings, update wage and employment figures, and rerun the ROI calculation. Submit a methodology memo with full documentation of data sources and decisions.

Build a county-specific input-output model in IMPLAN. Report the industry multipliers, tax revenue estimates, and how they compare to the flat 40% used in the 2017 analysis.
SLO 3 SLO 5 IMPLAN

Applied Research & Case AnalyticsData Intensive 35%
Three independent assignments develop your analytical skills alongside the consulting project. Each one asks you to take a position and defend it with evidence—a replication study that tests published findings, and two Harvard Business Publishing cases that apply economic reasoning to real decisions.
1
Journal Replication Project
Take a published study on housing and energy use, re-run the analysis on the original data, and write up what you find—including where the results hold and where they do not. This is not a summary; it is an audit. You are checking whether the authors’ conclusions survive your replication and, if they do not, explaining why.
SLO 3 — Methods & limitations SLO 5 — Software analysis
2
FTC v. Amazon — Harvard Business Case
Antitrust analysis using the HBP Case Companion. The question is whether Amazon’s pricing practices harm consumers or competition. Your memo must take a defensible position—not summarize both sides. Apply the relevant economic frameworks from the course and cite evidence from the case.
HBP Case Companion SLO 3 — Economic reasoning
3
Game Theory in Video Games — Harvard Business Case
Strategic decision-making applied to an industry where payoffs are measurable and the strategies are explicit. Identify the game structure, the dominant strategies (if any), and what the equilibrium outcome predicts. Connect the analysis to the game theory concepts from Weeks 8–9.
HBP Case Companion Ch. 15 — Strategic Games
AI reflection requirement. Each case submission includes a short reflection on how you used AI tools in the analysis—what you asked, what it produced, and what you changed. Details on the reflection format will be provided in class.
Applied Economics for the BSBA 30%
Monday sessions work through Managerial Economics (Froeb et al.). Each chapter starts with a real business decision and shows how economic thinking changes it. Problem sets and in-class assessments measure whether you can apply the framework, not just define it. The goal is to build a way of thinking that transfers to the consulting project, the cases, and your career.
—
Chapter Problem Sets
Problem sets follow the Monday lecture and are completed before the next class meeting. They are short—typically three to five applied problems drawn from the chapter’s business decision framework. Submissions are through Canvas.

In-Class Application Exercises
Brief in-class work that asks you to apply the week’s framework to a new decision. These are not announced in advance. They are completed in class and submitted at the end of the session. They measure whether you can use the tool, not whether you memorized the chapter.
