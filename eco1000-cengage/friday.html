The Economic Narrative

This assignment asks for something specific: a story from your own life, told carefully enough that a stranger could see it.

Not a summary of an economic concept. Not a report on something you read. A story, told in your own voice, about a moment when the economic forces you are studying this week were shaping what happened around you.

Your narrative should be 350 to 600 words of careful prose. It is not a journal entry and it is not a case study. It tells what happened, where, when, how, and why, with enough detail that someone who was not there can follow it.

The Specificity Requirement

Your narrative must be set in a specific, named, real place and time. Not "a store" but the actual store, the actual day, the actual circumstances. This is not a stylistic preference. It is a requirement. The specificity of your setting is the foundation of everything that follows in the viva conversation.

What Makes a Strong Narrative

A strong narrative has three qualities.

Brevity
Begin at the moment the story actually starts. Do not warm up with background your reader can figure out on their own. Every sentence earns its place.
Clarity
Tell events in the order they happened. Make the cause of each action visible: not implied, not assumed, but present on the page.
Credibility
Write characters and situations that are true to life. Your own experience is your authority here. The more specific and honest your account, the more credible it becomes.
The Economic Requirement

Your narrative must be grounded in a real experience you can speak to fluently. You should be able to answer from memory:

  • What did someone stand to gain or lose?
  • What did they give up to get it?
  • Was there a moment when a small change in price, in time, or in effort tipped the decision?
  • Who or what brought the pieces together?
You do not answer these questions in the writing. You answer them in the Narrative Viva. Your narrative does not need to use economic vocabulary.
Submission

Submit through Moodle as a single document, Word or PDF. Your name and the date at the top. Nothing else.

A note on artificial intelligence. You may use AI tools to help you think, outline, or revise. You may not use AI to write your narrative for you. If the narrative is not genuinely yours, the conversation will show it.

The Narrative Viva

A viva (pronounced VEE-vah) is a short conversation about a piece of writing you have already completed. Your Narrative Viva is a 12-minute Zoom conversation about the economic narrative you submitted for Friday Focus 1.

You will not be tested on definitions or asked to recite concepts. You will be asked about your story.

What I Am Looking For
Genuine Ownership of Your Story
You were there. No one else was. The specific, honest detail that only you have is the most important thing you can bring.
Economic Thinking, Not Economic Vocabulary
The concepts matter. The labels do not.
Honest Engagement
If you do not know the answer, say so. I am not timing your responses. I am listening to your thinking.
If you freeze, say so. "I need a moment" is a complete answer.
