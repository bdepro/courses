How To How to Make a Comment

Highlight text by clicking and dragging. Choose Annotate, or use the comment box that appears. Write your thought, question, or connection. Click Post.

To reply, click a classmate's comment and choose Reply. Use Upvote on comments that help you think.

Quality What Makes a Strong Comment

Strong comments in this course do one of four things. Perusall recognizes these as high-quality:

Ask a real question
"Why would demand shift here instead of moving along the curve?"
Explain in your own words
"This is basically saying incentives change behavior because..."
Make a connection
Link the story to supply and demand, inflation, unemployment, trade, or something from class.
Use evidence
Quote or point to a specific line and explain why it matters.

Perusall scores comments as high, medium, or low quality based on how much thinking your comment shows. High-quality earns full credit. Medium-quality earns partial credit. Low-quality earns no credit.

These earn little or no credit
  • "I agree."
  • "This is interesting."
  • Very short posts with no reason, example, or question.
Requirements Credit Checklist

To earn full credit for each newsletter:

  • 1 Post 2 high-quality comments. High-quality earns full credit. Medium-quality earns partial credit.
  • 2 Read to the end and complete the quiz. Scroll through the full newsletter and submit the short quiz at the end.
  • 3 Spend 30–45 minutes actively working. Active means reading, writing, replying, and thinking — not leaving the tab open.
  • 4 Open the assignment 3 times. Visit on at least two different days. Spreading your work improves both your learning and the discussion for everyone.
  • 5 Reply to at least one comment. A question works best — it keeps the conversation going.
  • 6 Receive at least one high-quality reply on your comment. Write a comment good enough that a classmate responds to it. A genuine question or a specific observation invites the best replies.
  • 7 Earn 1 upvote or give 3. Get one upvote on your work, or upvote three classmates whose comments helped you think.
