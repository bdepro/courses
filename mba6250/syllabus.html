Course Overview

Managers face a relentless stream of decisions — how much to produce, how to price, whether to enter a market, how to structure a contract, when to invest. Managerial Economics provides the analytical toolkit for making those decisions well.

This course applies microeconomic theory to the problems that practitioners actually encounter. The central method is problem solving: each topic is introduced through a managerial question, analyzed with a precise framework, and applied to real cases. The Froeb et al. textbook is deliberately lean and decision-focused — every chapter is organized around a type of decision, not an abstract theoretical concept.

By the end of the semester, you should be able to identify the economic structure of a business problem, apply the appropriate analytical framework, and communicate your reasoning clearly — in writing, in a case analysis, and in an executive memorandum.

AI in this course. AI tools are permitted and encouraged as analytical aids, particularly for case analysis. Any AI-assisted work must be disclosed. The policy is detailed on the AI Policy page. The Elon MBA's honor code applies to all submissions.
Learning Outcomes

Students completing this course will be able to:

  1. Apply marginal analysis to determine optimal quantities, prices, and investment levels in realistic managerial contexts.
  2. Analyze market structure — competitive, monopolistic, and oligopolistic — and identify the strategic implications for pricing and entry.
  3. Use game-theoretic reasoning to anticipate competitor behavior and evaluate strategic commitments.
  4. Structure bargaining problems and design contracts that align incentives across agency relationships.
  5. Identify the effects of information asymmetries (adverse selection, moral hazard) on market outcomes and organizational design.
  6. Communicate an economic analysis clearly and concisely in the format of a professional executive memorandum.
Assessments
Case Analyses (×10)
One case analysis per module. Each case presents a real or realistic managerial decision scenario. You will identify the economic problem, apply the chapter's framework, and recommend a course of action — with supporting analysis. Cases are submitted via Moodle. Length: 400–600 words plus any supporting quantitative work. AI disclosure required if used.
40%
4 pts each
Problem Sets (×10)
One quantitative problem set per module via Cengage MindTap. Problems are drawn from the textbook's end-of-chapter exercises and focus on the calculative and graphical skills underlying each framework. Graded automatically in MindTap; linked from the Modules page. Due by Friday at 11:59 p.m. each week.
30%
3 pts each
Executive Memo
A capstone deliverable due at the end of the course. Select a real managerial decision — a pricing change, market entry, acquisition, or organizational redesign — and produce a professional executive memorandum that applies at least three course frameworks to your chosen decision. Target audience: a senior executive who is not an economist. Length: 1,200–1,800 words. Prompt and rubric distributed in Module 5.
20%
20 pts

Course Policies

Late Work. Assignments submitted within 24 hours of the deadline receive a 20% penalty. Submissions more than 24 hours late receive a zero, except in documented emergencies. Contact me before the deadline if you anticipate a problem.

Academic Integrity. All work submitted for a grade must represent your own analysis. Collaboration is encouraged for understanding concepts; all submitted writing and calculations must be your own unless the assignment explicitly permits otherwise. Consult the Elon Honor Pledge and the MBA student handbook.

AI Tools. Use of AI is permitted with written disclosure. The disclosure statement must appear at the end of your submission and specify which tools you used, for what purpose, and what edits you made to the AI output. Undisclosed AI use is treated as an academic integrity violation.

Accessibility. Elon University is committed to providing an accessible learning environment. If you require accommodations, contact Disabilities Resources and provide your accommodation letter within the first two weeks of the semester.

Communication. I will respond to email within 48 hours on business days. For complex questions, schedule a Zoom office-hours appointment using the link on the course home page. Discussion-board questions that are broadly useful will be answered there so all students benefit.

Technical Requirements. Reliable internet access, a current browser, and access to Moodle and Cengage MindTap are required. Technical difficulties are not an automatic extension; contact me promptly if you encounter a persistent platform problem.
