50% Check-Ins

Four times during the semester you will take a 60-minute in-class Check-In on Wednesday, writing by hand. Each one covers the Hanley chapters from recent weeks, plus the standing analytical threads we develop together.

Check-In 4 always counts. If your CI 4 score beats your lowest score from CI 1, 2, or 3, I replace that score automatically. Miss a Check-In and CI 4 becomes your makeup. There are no separate makeup exams.

25% Friday Focus

Friday Focus asks one question: what do Hanley and Smith say about something you actually care about? You spend the semester building your answer. Write the narrative and submit it on Canvas by Wednesday, December 2 at 11:59 p.m. Then sign up for a viva slot during viva week. We will talk through what you wrote.

Sign up for your viva slot before Thanksgiving break. Slots fill. The Canvas link takes you straight to the scheduler.

15% Puzzles

Each content week has one Puzzle, a Canvas quiz due the Wednesday after that week at 11:59 p.m. You have two attempts. Canvas shows your score between attempts. Figure out where to spend your time, then come back for a sharper attempt.

There is no Puzzle in Week 7 (Mon–Tue only before Fall Break). That week has a Perusall reading but no quiz.

I drop your lowest puzzle score at the end of the semester. If you miss the Wednesday deadline, you have one more week to submit for 80% credit. After that, the puzzle closes.

10% Theory of Moral Sentiments

Four times during the semester you will read and respond to a passage from Adam Smith's Theory of Moral Sentiments. I introduce each one on the Friday of the designated week, due the following Wednesday at 11:59 p.m.

A strong response pushes back on the passage, asks what Smith left out, or connects his argument to something from Hanley. A weak response restates what the passage already said.
