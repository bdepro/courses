## Section III — Six Institutions and Policies That Promote Growth

*Comparison matrix — six factors, one shared logic: does this protect or erode the incentive to produce?*

**Connection to Section II:**
Trade, technology, and capital investment are the *sources* of growth. This section asks what has to be true about a country's legal and policy environment before those sources can actually operate.

| | What it protects or enables | What erodes it |
|---|---|---|
| Secure property rights & contract enforcement | The incentive to _____ and trade with strangers | Weak courts, expropriation risk |
| Competitive markets | Discipline that rewards businesses offering _____ value at low cost | Barriers to entry, protected incumbents |
| Stable money and prices | Predictable _____ prices, so contracts hold their value | Volatile inflation |
| Avoidance of high marginal tax rates | The incentive to earn one _____ dollar | Rates so high the next dollar isn't worth earning |
| Trade openness | Access to a broader _____ area | Tariffs, quotas, currency controls |
| Avoidance of entry-blocking regulation | The natural regulatory effect of _____ markets | Licensing and rules that protect insiders, not consumers |
