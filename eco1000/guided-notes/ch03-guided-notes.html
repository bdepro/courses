By the end of this lecture, I should be able to:
  1. Apply the laws of demand and supply to predict how buyers and sellers respond to a price change.
  2. Distinguish a change in demand/supply (a shift) from a change in quantity demanded/supplied (a movement), and identify which one a real scenario describes.
  3. Analyze how a market moves toward equilibrium, and evaluate why that point is also the most efficient outcome.
  4. Predict how a shift in demand or supply changes equilibrium price and quantity.
  5. Defend why market prices — without a planner — coordinate buyers' and sellers' decisions.

Pitfall Why it's wrong How to avoid it
