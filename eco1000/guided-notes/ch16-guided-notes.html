Section III  LT 3
Six Institutions and Policies That Promote Growth
Trade, technology, and capital investment are the sources of growth. This section asks what has to be true about a country's legal and policy environment before those sources can actually operate.
What it protects or enables What erodes it
Secure property rights & contract enforcement The incentive to and trade with strangers Weak courts, expropriation risk
Competitive markets Discipline that rewards businesses offering value at low cost Barriers to entry, protected incumbents
Stable money and prices Predictable prices, so contracts hold their value Volatile inflation
Avoidance of high marginal tax rates The incentive to earn one dollar Rates so high the next dollar isn't worth earning
Trade openness Access to a broader area Tariffs, quotas, currency controls
Avoidance of entry-blocking regulation The natural regulatory effect of markets Licensing and rules that protect insiders, not consumers
