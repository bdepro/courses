50% Check-Ins

Four times during the semester you will take a Check-In in class on Wednesday — pen and paper, 60 minutes, full class period. Each one covers the chapters from recent sessions, plus supply and demand analysis and the eight guideposts of economic thinking from Chapter 1.

Check-In 4 always counts. If your CI 4 score is higher than your lowest score from CI 1, 2, or 3, I replace that score automatically. If you miss a Check-In, CI 4 is the makeup. There are no separate makeup exams.

25% Friday Focus

Friday Focus asks one question: what does economics explain about something you actually care about? You spend the semester building your answer. The first part is a written narrative submitted as a Canvas assignment, due Monday, November 30 at 11:59 a.m. The second part is a viva — a short conversation with me about what you wrote, scheduled during viva week.

Sign up for your viva slot before Thanksgiving break. Slots fill. The Canvas link takes you straight to the scheduler.

15% Economic Puzzles

Each content session has one Economic Puzzle — a Canvas quiz due Monday at 11:59 a.m. You have two attempts. Canvas shows your score between attempts but not which questions you missed, so attempt two rewards genuine re-engagement.

I drop your lowest puzzle score at the end of the semester. If you miss the Monday deadline, you have one more week to submit for 80% credit. After that, the puzzle closes.

10% Monday Morning Economist

Four times during the semester you will read and annotate a current economics article through Perusall — one per check-in block. The Canvas link takes you straight to Perusall. No separate account needed. I introduce each article in class on the Friday of the check-in week. It is due the following Monday at 11:59 a.m.

A strong response pushes back on the article, asks what the author left out, or connects the argument to something from the course. A weak response restates what the article already said.
